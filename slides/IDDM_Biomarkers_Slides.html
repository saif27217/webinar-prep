Background

What is IDDM?

Insulin-Dependent Diabetes Mellitus is an autoimmune condition characterized by the selective destruction of pancreatic beta cells in the islets of Langerhans.

5-10% of all diabetes cases are Type 1
~8.4M people living with T1D worldwide
Peak incidence ages 4-6 and 10-14 years

Pathogenesis

1 Genetic susceptibility (HLA + polygenic)
2 Environmental trigger (viral, dietary)
3 Autoimmune activation (autoantibody appearance)
4 Insulitis and progressive beta-cell loss
5 Clinical diabetes (Stage 3)

Most SENSITIVE Adult Marker

GADA is the single best antibody to detect adult-onset autoimmune diabetes. In LADA (Latent Autoimmune Diabetes in Adults), GADA positivity is the defining criterion.

Clinical Pearl: Stiff-Person Syndrome

High-titer GADA (>2000 IU/mL) are found in Stiff-Person Syndrome (SPS), a rare neurological disorder. Up to 30% of SPS patients have concurrent T1D, and ~80% have GADA.

Detection Platforms

Radioimmunoassay (RIA) ELISA Electrochemiluminescence Simoa

Persistence

GADA can remain detectable for decades after diagnosis, making it useful for long-term autoimmune tracking and differential diagnosis.

Panel Impact

Adding ZnT8A to the standard 3-antibody panel increases detection from approximately 80% to 90% of at-risk individuals. The complete 4-antibody panel achieves >95% sensitivity.

Clinical Utility

ZnT8A is particularly useful in seronegative cases where the standard panel is negative but clinical suspicion remains high.

FDA Cleared

ZnT8A assays have received FDA clearance, validating their role in routine clinical screening for T1D risk.

Independent Prognostic Value

ZnT8A positivity independently predicts faster progression to Stage 3, even when adjusting for other autoantibodies and HLA status.

Target

Zinc transporter 8 (SLC30A8) mediates zinc transport into insulin secretory granules, essential for insulin crystallization and storage.

DR3/DR4 Heterozygotes

Heterozygous DR3/DR4 carriers have the highest genetic risk for T1D. This genotype accounts for approximately 30–40% of childhood-onset T1D.

Protective Haplotypes

DR2-DQ6 is strongly protective, reducing T1D risk by 90%+ through thymic negative selection of autoreactive T cells.

What PRS Captures

The Polygenic Risk Score aggregates effects from 60+ non-HLA GWAS loci including INS, PTPN22, CTLA4, IL2RA, IL7R, and others. Combined with HLA, it provides comprehensive genetic risk stratification.

Combined Risk Score (CRS)

CRS integrates PRS with autoantibody data, family history, and clinical features to achieve AUC ≥0.90 — outperforming any single biomarker for predicting 5-year progression to clinical T1D.

Functional Biomarker

C-Peptide — The Gold Standard

ONLY direct measure of beta-cell mass

<0.6 nmol/L
Threshold for severe deficiency

What is C-Peptide?

C-Peptide is cleaved from proinsulin during insulin biosynthesis. It is secreted in equimolar amounts to insulin, making it an accurate marker of endogenous insulin production.

MMTT (Mixed-Meal Tolerance Test)

The standard provocative test for residual beta-cell function. Patient ingests a standardized meal (Sustacal), and C-peptide is measured at 0, 30, 60, 90, and 120 minutes.

≥0.2 nmol/L: Adequate 0.1–0.2: Marginal <0.1: Undetectable

Clinical Applications

  • • Distinguishing T1D from T2D
  • • Monitoring disease progression
  • • Evaluating therapeutic efficacy
  • • Predicting hypoglycemia risk

C-peptide decline precedes clinical symptoms by months to years, enabling earlier intervention.

Frontiers

Emerging Biomarkers

PTM Autoantigens

Post-Translationally Modified

Citrullinated, deamidated, and hybrid insulin antigens show enhanced immunogenicity and may identify earlier autoimmune activation.

Proinsulin:C-Peptide

Ratio Biomarker

Elevated proinsulin:C-peptide ratio reflects defective proinsulin processing, indicating beta-cell stress before overt dysfunction.

ML + Multi-omic

Machine Learning Integration

Combining genomics, proteomics, metabolomics, and clinical data with ML models for personalized risk prediction exceeding traditional approaches.

T-cell Assays

Cellular Immunity

Antigen-specific T-cell activation assays (e.g., T-cell ELISpot) directly measure the effector arm of autoimmunity, complementing antibody-based approaches.

Endotyping

T1D Endotypes

Subclassifying T1D by autoimmune profile and metabolic phenotype to enable precision medicine approaches and targeted intervention.

Extracellular Vesicles

Exosome Cargo

Beta-cell-derived exosomes carrying miRNAs and autoantigens offer a liquid biopsy approach to monitor disease activity non-invasively.

Practice

Clinical Pearls

IA-2A vs GAD65: Complementary, Not Redundant

GADA has higher sensitivity; IA-2A has higher specificity. Together they cover more at-risk individuals than either alone. Always test both.

LADA: The Missed Diagnosis

~10% of adults diagnosed with T2D actually have LADA. GADA screening should be considered in any non-obese adult with apparently T2D, especially those progressing rapidly to insulin dependence.

ICI-Induced T1D: A Growing Concern

Immune checkpoint inhibitors can trigger autoimmune T1D as an adverse event. Monitoring GADA and C-peptide during ICI therapy enables early detection and intervention.

Standardization Matters

Autoantibody results vary significantly between laboratories. Always use IASP-standardized assays and reference sera. A positive result should be confirmed with a second method.

Guidelines

ISPAD 2024 Recommendations

1

Genetic Screening

Screen first-degree relatives of T1D patients with HLA genotyping and autoantibody panels.

2

Multi-Antibody Testing

Use at least 3 autoantibodies (GADA, IAA, IA-2A) for risk assessment; add ZnT8A when available.

3

Staging Monitoring

Regularly monitor at-risk individuals through staging with OGTT and C-peptide to detect Stage 2 early.

4

Teplizumab Consideration

Discuss Teplizumab (Tzield) for Stage 2 patients, particularly those with high-risk HLA and multiple autoantibodies.

5

Psychosocial Support

Provide comprehensive psychosocial support for individuals and families during the pre-symptomatic period.

6

Standardized Assays

Use IASP-standardized assays for all autoantibody testing to ensure consistent and comparable results.
